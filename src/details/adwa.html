Adwa

Adwa is a town in northern Ethiopia known for its historical significance. It is the site of the Battle of Adwa, which took place on March 1, 1896. During this battle, the Ethiopian Empire, led by Emperor Menelik II, successfully defeated the invading Italian army, becoming the first and only African country to do so during the period of European colonization. This victory was a major milestone in the struggle for African independence and is celebrated every year on March 1st as a national holiday in Ethiopia. Adwa is a popular tourist destination due to its rich history and cultural significance.

How To Get To Adwa

To get to Adwa, you can travel by car, bus, or plane. The town is located about 250 kilometers north of Addis Ababa and can be reached by driving, taking a bus, flying to the nearest airport in Mekelle and then taking a taxi or hiring a car to Mekelle and then continuing on by taxi or hired car. It is advisable to plan your trip in advance and book your transportation accordingly.
