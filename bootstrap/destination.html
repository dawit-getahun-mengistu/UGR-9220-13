Where Do You Want To Go?

Laibela
Lalibela is a small town in Ethiopia known for its incredible rock-cut churches, which were carved out of solid rock in the 12th century and are considered some of the most impressive architectural wonders in the world.
Aksum
Aksum is a historic city in Ethiopia known for its ancient ruins and cultural significance, including the Aksum Obelisk, which is one of the oldest and most important examples of ancient Ethiopian architecture.
Harar
Harar is a city in eastern Ethiopia known for its colorful houses, abundant markets, and rich cultural and historical significance as a center of Islam in the Horn of Africa.
Dalol
Dalol is a small town located in the Afar Region of Ethiopia, known for its hot and arid desert climate and the salt mines that are a major economic activity in the area.

What Do You Want to Celebrate?

Adwa
Adwa is a small town in Ethiopia known for its historical significance as the site of the Battle of Adwa, in which the Ethiopian army successfully defeated an invading Italian force in 1896.
Meskel
Meskel is a Christian holiday in Ethiopia that commemorates the finding of the True Cross by Empress Helena, the mother of Emperor Constantine, and is marked by colorful processions, bonfires, and other celebrations
Irrecha
Irrecha is a traditional Oromo festival in Ethiopia that involves praying, singing, dancing, and the sharing of food and drink, and takes place annually on the second Sunday of October at Hora Arsedi in Bishoftu.
Timket
Timket is a major Ethiopian Orthodox Christian festival that celebrates the Baptism of Jesus with elaborate ceremonies, including the blessing of holy water and the reenactment of the Baptism, and takes place on January 19th (or 20th).
